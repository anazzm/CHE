Before Disaster

Risk Assessment

Risk assessment involves identifying potential hazards, evaluating their likelihood and potential impact, and devising strategies to minimize or mitigate those risks. It's about understanding threats, analyzing their severity, and planning measures to reduce their adverse effects.

Preventive Measures

Preventive measures involve specific actions taken beforehand to minimize risks or avoid potential hazards. These actions aim to reduce vulnerability, such as securing buildings, creating emergency plans, and maintaining emergency supplies, to prevent or minimize the impact of disasters.

Education and Training

Education and training involve providing information and teaching skills related to disaster preparedness. This includes learning first aid, understanding survival techniques, and gaining knowledge about specific risks to enable individuals and communities to respond effectively to emergencies.

Community Engagement

Community engagement involves involving people and groups in collective efforts to prepare for disasters. It encourages collaboration, mutual support, and shared responsibility within neighborhoods or communities to enhance preparedness, response, and recovery from disasters.

Technological Solutions

Technological solutions refer to using tools, systems, or applications that leverage technology to enhance disaster preparedness and response. These solutions include early warning systems, mobile apps, or communication platforms designed to provide timely alerts, information dissemination, and support during emergencies.

Local Resources and Contacts

Local resources and contacts entail information about nearby emergency services, shelters, evacuation routes, and support networks available in a specific area during disasters. These details provide crucial contact information and resources for accessing assistance or guidance during emergencies.

After Disaster

Emergency Response and Safety

Emergency response and safety involve immediate actions taken post-disaster to ensure personal safety and mitigate further harm. This includes checking for injuries, securing the environment, and following safety protocols to safeguard lives and property in the aftermath of a calamity.

Assessment and Damage Evaluation

Assessment and damage evaluation refer to the process of inspecting property and infrastructure post-disaster to determine the extent of damage. It involves assessing structural integrity, evaluating property damage, and checking for potential hazards to understand the impact of the calamity.

Communication and Reporting

Communication and reporting involve methods of reaching out to emergency services and informing relevant authorities about post-disaster incidents. It includes utilizing communication channels to seek help, report emergencies, and share critical information with family, neighbors, and authorities for swift response and assistance.

Recovery and Restoration

Recovery and restoration encompass the steps taken post-disaster to rebuild and recover from the damage incurred. It involves initiating cleanup efforts, repairing infrastructure, and restoring affected areas to their pre-disaster state, aiming to restore normalcy and improve living conditions.

Financial and Legal Assistance

Financial and legal assistance involves accessing support and guidance post-disaster to handle financial matters and legal procedures. This includes seeking aid for insurance claims, accessing relief programs or grants, and obtaining legal assistance to navigate any legal complexities arising from the disaster.

Community Support and Engagement

Community support and engagement involve rallying together post-disaster to provide mutual assistance and aid in recovery efforts. This includes supporting neighbors, organizing community meetings, and participating in support groups or counseling services to foster unity and aid in the recovery process.

Learning and Preparedness for Future

Learning and preparedness for the future involve reflecting on the disaster experience to improve readiness for future events. This includes learning from the incident, updating emergency plans based on lessons learned, and enhancing disaster preparedness to better cope with similar situations in the future.

Long-Term Recovery and Resilience

Long-term recovery and resilience focus on sustained efforts post-disaster to rebuild and strengthen communities. This involves ongoing initiatives to recover from the disaster's long-lasting effects, improve infrastructure, and enhance preparedness to withstand future calamities, aiming for a more resilient and adaptive community.
